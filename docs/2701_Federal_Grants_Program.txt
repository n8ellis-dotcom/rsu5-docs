RSU5 Federal Grant Programs
Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA):
Each year RSU5 applies to the state for federal allocations under ESEA. These amounts are not in the local school budget, but are
approved (with other grants) under ARTICLE 21: EXPENDITURE OF GRANTS AND OTHER RECEIPTS. These amounts vary and
are set by the state. As the status for Federal funding for the State of Maine is unclear, estimates for the 2025-2026 school year have
NOT yet been provided by the Maine Department of Education. Last year we received approximately $239,591.99 under Title 1A
(disadvantaged students, used for supplemental instruction), $74,664.95 under Title IIA (teacher quality, used for professional
development), $12,954.83 under Title III (Language Instruction for Multilingual Learner) and $24,464.58 under Title IV (Student
support and academic enrichment.) The planning process for these projects occurs throughout the year. Public input is always
welcome, and the involvement of parents in Title 1A planning is a requirement. Final applications are typically made by August
which is the traditional deadline to apply for these funds.

Title 1A Project Title: Supplemental Instruction for Math and Literacy
1. Purpose: Students will receive specialized instruction to support their efforts to meet the Maine Learning Results in literacy and
mathematics.
2. ESEA Goals: All students will reach high standards, at a minimum attaining proficiency or better in reading language arts and
mathematics.
Objective 1 Increase the percentage of students who are at or above the proficient level in reading/language arts on state and local
assessments.
Objective 2 Increase the percentage of students who are at or above the proficient level in mathematics on state and local assessments.
Title 1A Project Description:
Identified Title 1 students will receive supplemental individual and small group instruction from the Title I teacher. The Title I teachers
will also consult with classroom teachers on the application of strategies to improve reading and math skills for identified students.
Title 1A Evaluation:
Progress will be measured through the use of locally developed standards attainment, standardized assessments, the Fountas and
Pinnell Benchmark Assessment 2.0 , and Maine Through Year Assessment. It is expected that at least 20% of the students who receive
supplementary instruction will exit at or above state standards.
