October 1, 2025
Dear Members of the RSU 5 Community,
As many of you may know, the State of Maine has directed all school districts to assume responsibility for identifying and providing a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) to children ages 3–5 with disabilities. This responsibility, previously held by Child Development Services (CDS), must be fully in place no later than July 1, 2028. While that date may seem far off, the change is significant and may ultimately affect how we use our facilities, allocate financial resources, hire and deploy staff, and plan for the long-term configuration of our district. There are also opportunities to begin earlier, with some financial support from the state, if we determine that’s the right path.
Rather than rushing into decisions or assuming what the right model looks like, we are beginning an exploratory and inclusive process. To do that, we are forming an Early Childhood Transition Task Force. This group will help us think carefully about the pace and approach that make the most sense for RSU 5, and how we can integrate early childhood services in a way that supports student growth from preschool through high school graduation.
A critical part of this conversation is the role of existing community child care providers. Our goal is to work collaboratively and respectfully with those who already serve young children, not to duplicate or displace what is already in place. We want to understand the landscape, listen carefully, and explore solutions that fill gaps and strengthen partnerships.
The charge of the Task Force is to develop a menu of options for the RSU 5 Board of Directors to consider as we craft a long-term, systemic plan that meets the needs of our youngest learners and their families. As part of that work, the Task Force will examine:
* Facilities and space across the district
* Staffing models and workforce needs
* Financial planning and sustainability
* Collaboration with community child care providers
* Impacts on district configuration, operations, and transitions for students

Ultimately, the RSU 5 Board of Directors will make the final decisions, but those decisions will be shaped by the recommendations and insights gathered through this community process.
To ensure broad representation and transparency, we are seeking participation from across the district. Specifically, we are asking for the following stakeholders:
   * One parent or community member from each town
   * One private care provider serving children ages 3 and 4 from each town
   * One Select Board or Town Council member from each town
   * One RSU 5 Board of Directors member from each town
   * Three RSU 5 staff members with expertise in early childhood education
These representatives will join district administrators to form an inclusive task force.
Meeting Schedule (5:30–7:30 p.m.):
   * October 29 – Morse Street School, Freeport
   * November 12 – Pownal Elementary School
   * December 18 – Durham Community School
   * January 7 – Freeport Middle School
If you are interested in serving, please send a letter of interest to me, Superintendent Tom Gray, c/o Ginny McManus, Assistant to the Superintendent, at mcmanusg@rsu5.org, no later than October 20 at 12:00 p.m. We will notify everyone who applies by October 22.
In your letter, please include:
   * Why you wish to participate
   * Your availability to attend all meetings listed above
   * The role you intend to represent (parent/community member, Board member, municipal official, staff, or provider)
   * The perspective or experience you would bring to the work
Your participation and voice are essential as we work together to thoughtfully plan for this responsibility. Our goal is to move forward transparently, collaboratively, and with great care for the children, families, and providers who are already part of this system.
Thank you for considering this opportunity to help shape the future of early childhood education in RSU 5.
