RSU5 Federal Grant Programs
Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA):
Each year RSU5 applies to the state for federal allocations under ESEA. These amounts are not in the local
school budget, but are approved (with other grants)under ARTICLE 17: EXPENDITURE OF GRANTS AND
OTHER RECEIPTS. These amounts vary and are set bythe state. Estimates for the 2021-2022 school yearare
about $214,685 under Title 1A (disadvantaged students, used for supplemental instruction), about $65,016
under Title IIA (teacher quality, used for professional development) and about $20,234 under Title IV (Student
support and academic enrichment.) (The planning processfor these projects occurs throughout the year. Public
input is always welcome, and the involvement of parentsin Title 1A planning is a legal requirement. Final
applications are made by August for the next school year.

Title 1A Project Title: Supplemental Instruction for Math and Literacy
1. Purpose: Students will receive specialized instructionto support their efforts to meet the Maine Learning
Results in literacy and mathematics.
2. ESEA Goals: All students will reach high standards, at a minimum attaining proficiency orbetter in
reading language arts and mathematics.
Objective 1 Increase the percentage of students who are at or above the proficient level in
reading/language arts on state and local assessments.
Objective 2 Increase the percentage of students who are at or above the proficient level in
mathematics on state and local assessments.
Title 1A Project Description:
Identified Title 1 students will receive supplemental individual and small group instruction from the Title I
teacher. The Title I teachers will also consult with classroom teachers on the application of strategies to improve
reading and math skills for identified students.
Title 1A Evaluation:
Progress will be measured through the use of locally developed standards attainment, standardized assessments,
the Fountas and Pinnell reading benchmark, and MAP Growth assessment. It is expected that at least 30%of the
students who receive supplementary instruction will exit at or above state standards.
