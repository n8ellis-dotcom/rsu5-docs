SECTION 1 – GENERAL INFORMATION
1.1 Purpose
A. Part One – Design and Engineering
RSU 5 School District is soliciting proposals from qualified Consultants to provide
professional design and consulting services for the following:
Freeport High School Tennis Courts. This work will provide all planning, engineering,
design, bidding documents, and project management working under the direction of RSU5
staff to provide critical analysis and design for tennis court replacements responsive to the
site and meets the needs of the community.
B. Part Two – Final Design Services
Upon successful completion of part one in this RFP and subject to obtaining sufficient
funding, the RSU5 intends to retain the successful consultant to provide final design and
contract administration services.
It is the intent of the RSU5 to review and assess the RFP responses to determine if the
responding firms can meet the needs of the School District.
1.2 General Submission Information
RSU5 intends to award a single contract for this project. The proposal should address the
Consultant’s capabilities for performing all aspects of the project development process
while presenting specific project information and substantiating the Consultant’s
methodologies and approach for completing the work requested. The official title for this
project is: Freeport High School Tennis Courts.
THE OFFICIAL TITLE MUST APPEAR ON ALL PROPOSALS AND RELATED
CORRISPONDENCE.

SECTION 3 – SCOPE OF WORK
3.1 Project Scope
The scope of professional services required by this RFP may include, but not necessarily be
limited to: See below;
A. Key Elements
1. Preparation of site survey including geotechnical investigation for base requirements
under new post tension tennis courts.
2. Removal and replacement of existing tennis courts.
3. Perimeter fencing.
4. Grade adjustments to incorporate new tennis courts into existing sites.
5. Restoration of turf, landscape, and sidewalks impacted by construction and other related
activities.
6. Tennis court and sidewalk grading to ensure proper drainage.
B. Design Services
1. Prepare plans specifications and supporting documents; prepare bidding and
construction documents; provide the advertisement for bids, a pre-bid meeting for
prospective bidders, and prepare bid tabulations and a recommendation for bid
award. Provide construction supervision, contract administration, and quality
control, for the facility until completed. Provide for and administer a 1 year warranty
period.
2. Conform to all purchasing and contract procedures of RSU5 for the construction of
the facility.
3. Coordinate as necessary with permitting agencies and utilities.

4. Comply with all Federal, State and local laws and guidelines pertaining to this
project. Comply with current ADA rules. Conform to applicable industry standards.
5. Provide all necessary printing and reproduction.

SECTION 5 – EVALUATION CRITERIA AND SELECTION PROCESS
Firms will be ranked using the points available for each RFP criterion. The criterion
categories and points available for each are as follows:
Category Points
Recent Parks and Recreation Project Management 35

Consultant Work Plan 25
Key Project Personnel 25
Past Project Performance 10
Grammar, Spelling and Conformance to RFP Instructions 5
Total Points 100
A committee of individuals representing RSU5 will evaluate the proposals. The committee
will rank the proposals as submitted.
RSU5 reserves the right to award contract(s) solely on the written proposal.
RSU5 also reserves the right to request oral interviews with the highest-ranked firms
(short-list).
The purpose of the interviews with the highest-ranked firms is to allow expansion upon the
written responses.
RSU5 reserves the right to request additional questions to be answered during the
interviews, determine the format and content of the interviews, and establish the maximum
number of people who attend the interview from each short-listed firm.
If interviews are conducted, a maximum of three firms will be short-listed.
The Consultant’s project manager identified in the proposal will be required to attend the
interviews.
By submitting a proposal, it is understood that the Proposers may not change (add or
delete) personnel for interviews from those listed in the proposals without written consent
RSU5.
A second score sheet will be used to score those firms interviewed. If oral interviews are
conducted, the final selection will be based on the total of all evaluators’ scores achieved on
the second rating. The highest-ranked Proposer(s), after the second scoring, if performed,

may be invited to enter into final negotiations with RSU5 for the purposes of Contract
award.
SECTION 6 – CONTRACT NEGOTIATION PROCESS
The highest-ranked Proposer(s) may be invited to enter into Contract negotiations with the
RSU5. If an agreement cannot be reached with the highest-ranked Proposer, RSU5 notify the
Proposer and terminate negotiations. The second highest Proposer may be contacted for
negotiations. This process may continue until successful negotiations are achieved.
However, RSU5 reserves the right to terminate negotiations with any Proposer should it be
in RSU5's best interest. The right is reserved to reject any or all proposals received, to waive
irregularities, and to accept that proposal which is in the best interests of the RSU5.
