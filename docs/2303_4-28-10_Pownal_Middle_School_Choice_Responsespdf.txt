REVISED
Pownal Middle School Choice Responses
April 28, 2010
An expectation of the budget development process is to consider the most effective use of
our financial resources while providing the strongest programming possible for our students.
Equity of educational opportunities had been a concern expressed by several parents about
Pownal’s middle school program. Parents have commented on a strengthened middle level
program this year; however, there are limited funds to increase student access to other offerings
and opportunities. The question to consider was how, in light of decreasing state revenues, we
can continue to provide varied academic, athletic, and co-curricular experiences to all of our
students while limiting the impact to taxpayers.
The initial recommendation was to replace Pownal’s current middle school program with
the choice of attending either Durham or Freeport middle school. Transportation would be
provided to either school. The goal of this recommendation was to save funds while continuing
to provide and enhance educational opportunities for our students. It was not an easy
recommendation to make but one that is focused on the best interests of students and taxpayers.
The RSU Board directed administration to ask families to consider their preferred choice
of school for their child’s middle school program for next year. All three middle schools were
offered as options. Transportation would be provided to the schools but we do not anticipate
providing transportation for after-school activities.

Considerations:
As outlined at the last meeting, the Board is being asked to consider a vote on one of the
following options:
1. Keep all Pownal students at Pownal Middle School;
2. Eliminate the Pownal Middle School program and allow Pownal students in grades 6-8
the choice of Freeport or Durham middle school programs with transportation provided;
3. Allow Pownal students in grades 6-8 the choice of attending school at Pownal, Freeport,
or Durham.
