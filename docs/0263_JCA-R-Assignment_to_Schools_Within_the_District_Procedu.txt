ASSIGNMENT TO SCHOOLS WITHIN THE DISTRICT
Each student in the district is required to attend the school designated for the geographic
attendance area in which he or she resides. A parent or guardian may request that his or
her child be allowed to attend another school in the district. Requests must be submitted,
in writing, to the Superintendent of Schools.
Transfers may be granted if:
A. An educational, safety, or health condition affecting the student would be
substantially improved as a result of the transfer; or
B. There is some other special hardship or detrimental condition affecting the student
or the student’s immediate family which would be alleviated as a result of the
transfer. Special hardship or detrimental condition includes, but is not limited to,
the following:
1. A student who moves during the school year from one attendance area in
the district to a new attendance area in the district may elect to transfer at
the time of the move or at the end of the semester or grading period.
2. A high school freshman, sophomore or junior who requests to transfer
from a choice school into the RSU high school may be asked to have the
transfer coincide with the beginning of a new grading period.
Transfer requests will be considered using the following criteria:
A. Whether space is available in the grade level or classes at the building in
which the student desires to be enrolled;
B. Whether appropriate educational programs or services are available to
improve the student’s condition as stated in requesting the transfer;
C. Whether the student’s transfer is likely to create a risk to the health or safety
of other students or staff at the new building; and
D. Any other pertinent criteria justified by circumstances at the time of
consideration of the request.
No assignment to schools or attendance areas shall discriminate against students on the
basis of gender, race, religion, disability, or national origin.
School bus transportation will not be provided for students attending schools outside their
attendance area unless they can be accommodated on existing bus routes and schedules;
or an exceptional child is involved; or unless specific permission is granted by the Board.
Administrative Procedure Adopted: November 10, 2009
