THE SCHOOLS
RSU5 is created from the communities of Durham, Freeport and Pownal. Freeport High School regularly
scores in the top 10% of all Maine high schools on standardized tests. We encourage our students to
look beyond those numbers and create and embrace their own learning goals.
Currently, the RSU5 schools serve 1,993 students in grades PreK through grade 12. The district operates
six schools, specifically a PreK-5 Elementary School in Pownal, a PreK-8 school in Durham, and PreK-2, 3-
5, 6-8 and 9-12 schools in Freeport.

ACCOUNTABILITY INFORMATION
The Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA), requires all states, school districts, and schools to
provide annual report cards to parents and communities. Maine’s ESEA Report Cards include Maine
Educational Assessment (MEA) data disaggregated into subgroups, additional accountability indicators
(e.g., graduation rates), and information about teacher qualifications. This report card also displays
statewide academic achievement results in grades four and eight on the National Assessment of
Educational Progress (NAEP) reading and mathematics assessments. The State will release this data in
June. Once released, it will be available on the RSU5 website at www.rsu5.org/administration2/28 for
your review.
DISTRICT SCORECARD
The RSU Scorecard provides information on academic achievement and academic growth throughout
the district. RSU5 uses the MAP Growth assessment often referred to as the NWEA (Northwest
Evaluation Association) assessment, a research-based computerized assessment, as a measure to
determine academic growth and academic achievement in literacy and math across the District in
grades 3-10. Please visit www.rsu5.org/administration2/28 to review the report cards.

Morse Street School
by Julie Nickerson
Last year at Morse Street School, our budget focused on
strengthening our support for early intervention and RTI (Response to
Intervention) by adding a Math RTI teacher. During this current year,
we have continued to build our RTI supports with a focus on the social-
emotional needs of our students. We added 0.25 social work time.
This has enabled us to provide more targeted and sustained support to
students with social-emotional and behavioral needs. Our social
worker works collaboratively with classroom teachers and our school
counselor to develop and implement support plans for students.
As a team, we are able to identify skill deficits and our social
w orker provides targeted group work on skill building so that
these students can be more successful in the classroom.
This year as we continue to develop our RTI (Response to
Intervention) support system for students with social emotional
and behavioral needs, we are requesting the addition of an ed
tech III. Unlike our social worker, this person will be in our
building full time, helping students with both preventative and
responsive support, so that students are available for learning
In the classroom. This person will work in collaboration with our social worker to ensure student
support plans are in place and are consistently implemented. Early intervention is important so that
gaps can be filled and students can be successful as they move through the K-12 system.
Due to our increasing enrollment, we are requesting an additional first grade teacher. Our district
has made a commitment to keep class sizes small in line with the class size policy. Currently, we
have 87 students enrolled in kindergarten who are divided into 5 classes in kindergarten.
Additionally, we are requesting an additional PreK teacher based upon parent requests and need.
We look forward to welcoming more of our youngest learners to Morse Street School!

Durham Community School
by Will Pidden
This year the students of Durham Community School have benefitted from the additional staffing
that was added in last year’s budget. A very visual example of this is the impact of adding additional
time for our band/chorus teacher. We added one and half days of time, which is spread out over
the week, and has resulted in a 30% increase in the size of both the band and chorus. This
additional time also resulted in more lesson time for band students. We have enjoyed the concerts
so far this year and look forward to continued growth moving forward.
We also added a half time literacy specialist to support students in grades 5-8. This had been an
unfilled need at DCS as our literacy support only went through 4th grade and this position has
helped support students right through our range of grades. The literacy specialist works as part of
our RTI process, focused on helping all students be proficient across the content areas.
Throughout the year, our teaching staff have been involved in ongoing high level professional
development and training. The model of embedding the training into the school day with the
support of our coaches and curriculum consultants allows the staff to work with colleagues across
the district and with students at the same time. Additional staff meeting time after school, which
allows teachers additional o pportunity to apply their new learning, has supplemented this work.
Looking ahead to next year, we are going to build upon the success of this year utilizing the
resources we have, but are requesting an additional teaching position in response to a bubble class
of students who will be in 6th grade next year. There is also a request for some additional book
money to refresh some of the classroom libraries that are at the heart of the reading and writing
curriculum. Having “just right” books in the hands of students at all times is an integral component
in the process of building reading skills and stamina.
These budget requests will support continuous improvement at Durham Community School.

Freeport High School
by Jen Gulko
Freeport High School received support through this year’s budget to include a part time math
coaching position. The addition of this position has enabled us to support teachers through targeted
instructional coaching with the goal of continuously enhancing student success. We were also able to
add stipends for each of our department chairs which has supported them in leading professional
development meetings for our faculty. The budget for this school year also supported our NEASC
accreditation process, which took place in October of 2018.
Our budget for the 2019-2020 school year includes increases in teaching positions in science, World
Language and our Endeavor Program. We have requested a 0.25 increase to a science teacher position,
which will allow us to continue to provide support for our students in our science courses. We have
requested a 0.5 increase in the World Language department, as this will enable us to continue to offer
higher levels of language courses for our students. We have requested a 1.0 increase for a teacher in
our Endeavor Program, which is an alternative pathway for FHS students. Currently, this program
supports 11th grade students and the increase in the position will enable us to expand the program to
support students in other grade levels.
We believe that the budget has supported Freeport High School in past years to implement programs
and staffing increases that help our students continue to reach high levels of success. The requests that
we have made for the coming school year will enable us to continue to expand upon the supports that
we have in place in order to enhance student achievement.
