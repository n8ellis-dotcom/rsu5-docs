Mandate: July 1, 2028
On July 1, 2028 RSU 5 will be responsible to identify and to provide Special Education services to young learners 3 - 5 years of age.
“Responsibility for Part B [Special Education for Students aged 3-5 yo], Section 619. Until June 30, 2028, the commissioner shall designate
responsibility for child find activities and for ensuring a free, appropriate public education for children eligible under Part B, Section 619 to the
Child Development Services System or to school administrative units in accordance with the transition schedule [“Cohorts”] and supports [Funding
and Technical Assistance] under section 7209-A. Beginning July 1, 2028, the commissioner shall designate responsibility for child find activities
and for ensuring a free, appropriate public education for children eligible under Part B [Special Education for Students aged 3-5 yo], Section
619 to school administrative units.”
(Sec. W-11. 20-A MRSA §7006)
Maine DOE has recently renamed “Part B” [Special Education for Students aged 3-5 yo] to “Early Childhood Special Education”

What does this mean?
All Maine districts are mandated to identify students with disabilities
(Child Find) and provide Free and Appropriate Education extended to 3 - 5
year olds by July 2028

When Joining A Cohort…
RSU 5 assumes:
1.) Responsibility to provide or contract to provide all services determined in
IEPs for students selected to serve: all 3-5 yo or solely 4-5 yo for the
coming year.
2.) Legally responsible for any complaints brought forth specific to the a lack
of service provision.
3.) Freedom to design, implement, and maintain a plan to meet the mandate
while receiving the support: subsidizes a budget over two years @100% of
actual cost based on an allocation model.

Definitions
Child Find - All Maine districts are mandated to identify students with disabilities (Child Find) Free and Appropriate Public Education
(FAPE) - Provision of Free and Appropriate Education (access through Special Education to General Education)
Office of Special Services and Inclusion Education (OSSIE) The Office of Special Services and Inclusive Education is oversees the
provision of a free, appropriate public education in the least restrictive environment for children with disabilities (ages 3 to 22) as well
as early intervention services to infants and toddlers (birth through age 2).
Child Development Services
- Early Intervention, CDS Part C serves children birth to 2 years of age
- Early Childhood Special Education, CDS Part B serves children 3 -5 years of age
Individual Education Plan (IEP) written, legally binding plan for a student with a disability who qualifies for special education services,
outlining their current performance levels, annual academic and functional goals, and the specific special education, related services, and
supports they will receive to ensure they make progress in the general curriculum.
