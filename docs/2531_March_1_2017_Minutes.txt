Freeport High School
Project:
March 1, 2017 at 7:30 a.m.
Date:
Attendees: Lyndon Keck*, John Simoneau/Committee Chairman, Ben Jamo, Becky Foley, Superintendent/RSU 5,
Jen Gulko, Principal/FHS, Dennis Ouelette/Owner’s Rep., David Smail/FHS, Kim Lamarre/Committee

Purpose: Monthly Building Committee Meeting
These notes were prepared by Lyndon Keck to the best of his ability. If there are any oversights
please notify PDT Architects within three (3) working days.
Topic Agenda/Notes Action
1. The meeting was called to order at 7:35 a.m.
2. The Building Committee read and reviewed the February 1, 2017 Building
Committee meeting notes. A motion was made by Becky Foley and
seconded by Dennis Ouelette to approve the meeting notes. The meeting
notes were approved unanimously.
3. Chairman Simoneau gave a construction update. He explained there was
an invoice requisition submitted with a current payment due of $119,050. It
will be reviewed at the next pay requisition meeting on March 7, 2017 by the
architect and construction team.
4. This invoice brings the percent complete up to 92.8%.
5. Dennis Ouelette gave a brief construction update. He explained the work
was going well. Most of the framing was done in the administrative office
area, as well as the new art rooms and new IT work/conference rooms.
Dennis offered to give Committee members a quick peek at the work after
the meeting.
6. Dennis explained work was “on-going” in terms of metal stud framing,
electrical, conduit, wiring and HVAC ductwork. He also explained the
windows were kept on the north side of the cafeteria to let more natural light
into the art rooms.
7. Dennis explained that asbestos tile and mastic had been removed in the two
major hallways at the 1955 building over the February vacation break.

A Committee member asked about the strong odor that was permeating the
hallways. He explained that staff and students were concerned the odors
were not toxic or harmful. Dennis and Becky Foley explained they had
reviewed the MSDS chemical sheets. There does not appear to be any
harmful side effects as a result of using the chemical strippers, although the
odor was strong. Dennis explained fans had been used and doors will be
kept open to try to air out the hallways.
8. David Smail shared a small bag of asbestos tile samples which he found
under a bank of lockers. He asked if there was still asbestos and chemical
residue under the lockers that should be considered harmful. Becky and
Dennis stated that the asbestos abatement contractors would have to come
back and do additional cleaning under the lockers. The loose,
miscellaneous scraps of tile would not pose a hazard because the tile and
mastic were not friable.
Dennis and Becky explained they would continue to try to air out the
hallways to reduce the odor as soon as possible. They will check with Mark
Coleman, the hazardous material hygienist, concerning any possible health
effects related to residue.
9. Lyndon explained that PDT was not directing that scope of work but it was
his understanding that the chemical odor is resulting from a chemical stripper
used to remove the black mastic from the flooring. The mastic is not friable
or dangerous; it is best removed chemically rather than grinding which would
make it friable and create dust in the environment.
10. John Simoneau gave a brief update on the track and field status. He
explained that the Committee had issued an RFQ to contractors requesting
qualifications from possible bidders.
John explained they would be going to the Project Review Board on the
evening of March 1st to get approval for the project. He explained the
Project Review Board had determined that they did not have authorization to
permit sports lighting in excess of poles higher than 20’. The Committee will
be working with the Project Review Board, Planner and Town Council to
have a special sports lighting ordinance implemented.
11. Traffic and parking had been raised as an issue. The Committee is
addressing events parking.

12. There was a question asked if there were good contractors interested in
installing the track and field. John explained they expected to get several
good, qualified firms to submit RFQ’s who would want to participate in the
bidding.
13. Lyndon passed out a change proposal log showing recent approved items,
as well as upcoming items that PDT knew about. Lyndon explained that
Change Request #60RR and Change Request #89 were two items which
were in dispute between Sheridan Corporation and PDT Architects. Lyndon
explained PDT does not automatically approve every request from Sheridan
Corporation. We review all proposed change orders to determine if the price
seems reasonable. Lyndon explained Request #60RR was a disagreement
between the mason and PDT Architects concerning increasing the thickness
of the CMU wall at the Fitness Rooms. The mason was requesting $9,078;
PDT thought the price was closer to $1,500-$2,000.
Change Request #89 was at the knuckle between the gym, performing arts
center, new building and the 1955 building. Sheridan was requesting
$15,678. Lyndon explained that PDT had analyzed this and felt some of the
work was related to mistakes Sheridan had made. PDT thought the price
that was approvable was closer to $10,000.
Lyndon explained he wanted the Committee to know there were at least
these two outstanding items which were unresolved between Sheridan
Corporation and PDT Architects
14. The question was asked if the security cameras had all been installed.
Dennis explained all the inte rior cameras were now installed but there were
two or three exterior cameras which had not yet been installed.
15. There was a brief discussion about whether or not existing lockers in the old
building should remain or be taken out. This also included a brief discussion
of how many lockers are possibly needed. It was agreed that Dennis, Jen,
and Becky would report out on administration’s recommendation for how
many lockers should be left in the school and where they should be located
at the April meeting.
16. Jen asked Lyndon why the refrigerator had not been installed in the new
teacher’s room. Lyndon explained he will get an answer.
17. A question was asked if any more work was going to be done related to
putting lights on the high school signage. Comments were made that there
was some light on the west elevation facing Interstate 295. Perhaps there
