﻿FHS HABITS OF WORK (HoW)        Created 2019                                                 2022 - 2023


The FHS Habits of Work rubric and scores will be used school-wide.


Purpose: At Freeport High School we strongly believe that a student’s success and achievement is closely related to a strong work ethic, persistence and other positive classroom behaviors.  Teachers will support students in learning and developing strong work habits and will provide feedback on student progress through the FHS Habits of Work Rubric.  At FHS, Habits of Work scores reflect the belief that:
* Students can develop behaviors which support their own academic and interpersonal growth.
* Students will enhance skills that will directly impact their success in college and/or career.
* Students can potentially earn more time and support in order to further develop understanding and/or re-assess when necessary when demonstrating strong work habits.
* Student work habits should be tied to co curricular activity (see athletic handbook).


Habits of Work Criteria:
Preparedness: Timeliness, Organization, Homework Completion
Engagement: Participation, Collaboration, Respect, Leadership
Work Ethic: Meets Criteria, Effort and Attention to Detail, Quality of Work, Perseverance


Interpretation:  We recognize that each class has different qualities, and so augmenting these indicators or interpreting them to make the most sense in one’s classroom is encouraged. All teachers should use the common rubric and then share details with students about specific course and teacher expectations. These HoW qualities are purposefully left open to interpretation.  


Frequency/Timeframe:
Each teacher will give students a HoW score on at least once (on any indicator) every two weeks and the score will be entered in PowerSchool.  All three indicators must be assessed and put into PowerSchool at least once per month.  Teachers may give HoW scores more frequently than that, but at a minimum, students will receive a HoW score once every two weeks.


Habits of Work and Reassessment:
In our school, students can earn opportunities for reassessment. Each course will have a clearly outlined process for how and when a student is eligible to reassess.  Teachers of each course will inform students of their reassessment process.  All tasks associated with the relearning process are at the discretion of the teacher.
Although reassessment can/will look different in each course, there are some school-wide processes that we’ll use:
* A student will have the option to request to retake or revise a summative assessment if s/he has not yet reached proficiency (83 or higher). 
* Teachers decide whether a student is eligible to participate in the reassessment process. 
   * Habits of Work grades are factored into this decision. 
* Students who do not request or do not complete the reassessment opportunity will maintain the original score.
* There are no reassessments for midterm and final exams.
* There are no opportunities for reassessment for incidents of academic dishonesty.
Reporting: 
* HoW grades will be reported on PowerSchool, on grading reports, and on student transcripts.
* In addition to an academic honor roll, there will be HoW recognition.
