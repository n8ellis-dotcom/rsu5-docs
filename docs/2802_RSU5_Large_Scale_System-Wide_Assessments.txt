	Developmental Indicators for the Assessment of Learning (DIAL-4)
	Before Kindergarten to all students who did not have PK
	20-30 minutes
	Screens five areas: motor, language, basic concepts, self-help, and social development.
	This is a quick, norm-referenced early screening test, composed of a variety of fun tasks.  In some cases, results could indicate a need for assistance or intervention.
	Before Kindergarten
	Benchmark Assessment System (BAS), Fountas and Pinnell
	Kindergarten through 
Grade 8
	20-50 minutes 
	Measures independent and instructional reading levels, including fluency, comprehension, accuracy, and rate.
	Students are provided with books that match their reading levels, to ensure they are challenged and successful in becoming readers. Students may be identified as needing further assistance in learning to read, or qualified for programs that provide assistance. Results may be used to determine if class, school, or district goals in reading have been met, and  inform other decisions.

	Maine Through Year Assessment 


	Grades 3-8
and 2nd year in high school.
	Approx. 2 sessions of 
60 minutes each (computer adaptive)
	Measures the progress of students toward the content standards adopted for Maine students in literacy and mathematics.
	Scores may be used to make informed decisions that connect assessment to instruction.  They are used to measure the progress of each class and school, to make changes in instruction or curriculum, and to determine the success of improvement efforts. This assessment will be used to meet the federally required assessment that determines school success under the ESSA.
