Mandated Transfer of Early Childhood Special Education
No later than July 1, 2028…
RSU 5 will be responsible to conduct Child Find for children ages 3 -22 residing
within district limits and provide a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) to
all students 3-4 years of age with active Individualized Education Plans (IEP), in
accordance with Sec. W-11. 20-A MRSA §7006
Task Force Results

Cohort 3 Decision
Based on RSU 5’s thorough process over the last few months, district
leadership, the current Early Intervention Team (EIT), and the Early Childhood
Education Task Force have unanimously voted to join Cohort 3*.
* Cohort 3 is comprised of school districts taking on the transfer of Child Find
and FAPE beginning in 2026 - 2027.

+ Dedicated staffing: 1 Special Education Teacher and 3 Ed Techs
+ Provides the district an option for special purpose level programming

+ Staff, materials, and equipment are fully covered through two funding
streams at no cost to district
Special Education Classroom (SC)
The Special Education classroom is designed as part of a continuum of services
ranging from least to most restrictive supports. It provides flexible levels of
intervention that can be adjusted based on individual student needs. When
appropriate, the classroom can function as a self-contained setting to support
students with the most complex needs, allowing the district to meet those
needs in-district rather than seeking more restrictive outside placements.
