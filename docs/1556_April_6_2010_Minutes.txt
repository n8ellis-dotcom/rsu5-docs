Strategic Planning Subcommittee Report
Date: April 16, 2010
Committee: Strategic Planning
Meeting Date: April 6, 2010
Members Present: Eric Dube, Betsy Peters, and Shannon Welsh
With Diane Boucher, Finance Director and David Ruff, Great Schools
Agenda Items and Discussion:
Review of Proposed Financial Indicators:
The draft Strategic Plan Financial Indicators document was presented for review and
consideration by the Strategic Planning Committee. Members of the Financial Indicators group
included Rod Regier from Freeport; Allan Purinton from Durham; Cliff Ruprecht from Pownal;
Lois Kilby-Chesley representing our teachers; Diane Boucher and Shannon Welsh. The
document provides detailed information pertaining to each of the strategic goals. In addition, the
group recommended an additional Goal to evaluate and maintain financial health of the
organization. The Strategic Planning Committee provided suggested changes to the document to
have two additional years of information on the square footage costs by students (goal 1). They
discussed the use of weighted averages and comparable systems. Several format changes were
also recommended. Overall, the committee was very pleased and appreciative of the work
completed by the Financial Indicators group. This document will provide the baseline data for
documentation of our finances over time. The Board had anticipated adoption of this work at the
meeting on April 28th but the Strategic Planning Committee is recommending this be moved to
May 12th due to the need to focus on the proposed 2010-2011 budget.
Update on Performance Indicators meetings:
David Ruff, Great Schools, presented a summary of information developed as a result of the
Performance Indicator Task Force work on March 11th. A follow-up meeting is scheduled for
April 12th to review the recommendations and consider additional questions that they didn’t have
time to address at the first meeting. David walked the Strategic Planning Committee through the
draft report and explained why recommendations were made. Members discussed the
recommendations and raised additional questions they would like the Task Force to consider.
There has been very positive feedback from members of the Task Force about the process used
to consider additional performance indicators. Teachers, parents, administrators, and students
are represented on the Task Force. The quality of discussion and work from the group is
exceptional and greatly appreciated.

It is also recommended that the final recommendations be presented to the Strategic Planning
Committee at their next meeting scheduled for May 4th at 5:30 p.m. at the Central Office. We
anticipated finalizing the document for presentation to the Board at their May 12th meeting.
