Agenda Items and Discussion:
Preliminary information for the State of the Schools report
Joe Makley presented the early data gathered to assess the RSU5 Strategic Plan, including NECAP and
MHSA scores, Financial data, College attendance, etc. The data will be published in the State of the
Schools report, to be mailed to every household in March.
Strategic Plan Update (Standards-based)
Joe Makley described the progress toward the implementation of a standards-based system. The
elementary schools are already using standards-based curricula in math and reading, will be
implementing writing formally this fall. The middle schools and the high school are redesigning units
and courses to improve rigor and alignment with the new Common Core State Standards, in
preparation for the “Smarter-Balanced” computer-based assessments that will replace the NECAPs
during the 2014-2015 school year. The high school is working to clarify the “pathways,” or options
for students to achieve a “proficiency-based” diploma by 2018.
Communications/ Education Forum:
The committee discussed options for obtaining public participation and feedback on the State of the
Schools report. It was decided to hold three “Dine and Discuss” sessions, one in each of the towns.
Topics will include the State of the Schools report, the 2013-2014 Proposed Budget and the Freeport
High School Renovation Project. Conversations will take place from 5:30-6:30 p.m. on the following
dates:
Wednesday, April 10, 2013 – Pownal Elementary School
Wednesday, April 24, 2013 – Durham Community School
Wednesday, May 1, 2013 – Freeport High School
