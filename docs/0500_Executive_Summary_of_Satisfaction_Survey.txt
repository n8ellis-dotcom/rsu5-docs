Executive Summary
2018 Satisfaction Survey
June 13, 2018
In February of 2018, RSU5 conducted a Satisfaction Survey to provide a means of input for
parents and community members to identify areas of strength as well as areas for growth
in our schools.
We received a total of 355 surveys which is approximately 18% of the families in the
District. This assumes that only one person in the family completed the survey. Below is a
summary of the most significant findings by district and school level. At the comment level,
topics that appeared most frequently are noted.
Districtwide Results (355 surveys (18% of families) completed):
The following questions received 75% or more of strongly agreed/somewhat agreed:
● The school is clean and well-maintained *
● When reporting about my child’s progress, teachers use language I can understand
● The school reflects the pride taken in educating our students
● The school provides quality teaching/learning materials and technology equipment
● I feel comfortable talking with my child’s teacher(s)
● The school offers a broad range of co-curricular activities
● The atmosphere at school is positive and conducive to learning
● The school encourages students to show respect for each other
● Adults who work in the school treat students with respect
● I feel welcome at my child’s school
● Parents have opportunities to participate in school activities
● Behavior expectations are made clear to students
● The school is safe and orderly
* Any question that received 90% or above has an asterisk at the end of the question.
The following questions received 20% or more of strongly disagreed/ somewhat
disagreed:
● My child learns life skills such as managing time, prioritizing, meeting deadlines,
responsibility, and communicating clearly.
● Teachers meet my child’s needs by including a variety of teaching strategies and
activities
● My child receives additional challenges as needed
● Special education services meet the needs of my child
● GT programming meets the needs of my child
● Teachers assign high-quality homework that supports learning
● Administrators and teachers communicate with parents/guardians effectively
● Teachers and administrators are responsive to my concerns and suggestions
● Teachers help me understand how my child’s work is evaluated
● My child’s emotional needs are understood and properly addressed
● The school helps me understand how to best support my child’s learning at home
