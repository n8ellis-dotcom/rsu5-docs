Annual Budget Meeting
May 24, 2017
Freeport High School Gymnasium
Board Chair Michelle Ritcheson officially called to order the Annual Budget Meeting for
Regional School Unit No. 5 at 6:35 P.M. and provided a brief overview of the budget.
Pledge of Allegiance
Article 1A: Board Chair Ms. Ritcheson nominated Mr. Gregory Im for Moderator. Board
member Ms. Parker seconded the nomination. No further nominations occurred
and Mr. Im was elected by unanimous vote. Superintendent Foley swore in Mr.
Im.
Mr. Im welcomed all to the meeting and explained the following:
Must be a registered voter to vote. Raise your card to vote.
The packets of budget information are located in the entry way;
Recording Secretary is Cynthia Alexander;
Mr. Im asked to allow the following administrators in RSU5 who live outside Durham,
Freeport or Pownal be allowed to speak at this meeting: Will Pidden, Hiram Sibley, Julie
Nickerson, Jen Gulko, Lisa Demick, Kim Austin, Cynthia Alexander, Bonnie Violette,
Dennis Ouellette, Seth Thompson, Darren Carter and Michelle Lickteig. There were no
objections to letting these people speak.
The Maine Moderators Manual will be used as a guideline.
Speakers must come to the center microphone for discussion, state their name and town of
residence;
If you wish to end debate on a motion say, "I move the previous question". This requires a
2/3 vote;
There will be no amendments to amendments to motions;
Vote will be by show of cards;
Article 15 requires a written ballot.

ARTICLE 15: Ms. Ritcheson moved, Ms. Parker seconded to see what sum the Regional
School Unit will raise and appropriate in additional local funds
(Recommend $8,518,194), which allow this budget to exceed the State's
Essential Programs and Services allocation model by (Recommend
$7,615,841.15 as required to fund the budget recommended by the School
Board.
The School Board Recommends $8,518,194 for additional local funds and
gives the following reasons for exceeding the State's Essential Programs
and Services funding model by $7,615,841.15. The State's Essential
Program and Services funding formula is a minimum model to support
education in grades K-12. RSU No. 5 provides additional funds to support
lower student/teacher ratios and additional co-curricular activities in
addition to complete educational programs for all our students.
Moderator suggests that amounts recommended may be increased to
adjust for increase to Article 6.
Moderator explains that this would bring the new amount exceeding
the State's Essential Program and Services funding formula to
$7,689,209.15.

Parker second.
