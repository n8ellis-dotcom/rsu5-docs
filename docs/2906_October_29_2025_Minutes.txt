Agenda
5:30 – 5:40 | Welcome and Introductions
● Superintendent’s opening remarks and purpose of the Task Force.
● Roundtable introductions (name, role, and connection to early childhood education).
● Overview of meeting norms and collaboration expectations;
○ 1. Stay Student-Centered
Keep our focus on what will best serve children and families — especially our youngest
learners — as we explore options for the district’s future responsibilities.
○ 2. Honor All Perspectives
We come from different roles and experiences. Listen with curiosity and respect; seek to
understand before seeking to be understood.
○ 3. Assume Positive Intent
Everyone in the room is here because they care about children and want to do what’s right.
Approach differences in opinion with generosity.

○ 4. Speak from Experience, Listen for Understanding
Use “I” statements to share your perspective. When others speak, listen to understand, not to
reply.
○ 5. Be Present and Engaged
Limit side conversations, multitasking, and phone use. We have important work to do in a
short time.
○ 6. Keep It Constructive and Solution-Oriented
Our charge is to identify possibilities and options — not to assign blame. Let’s focus on what
we can do and what’s within our control.
○ 7. Be Mindful of Time and Space
Help ensure everyone’s voice is heard by being concise when speaking and inviting quieter
voices in.
○ 8. Transparency and Trust
We will share information openly and ensure that processes are clear to all. What we learn
together will be communicated accurately and respectfully.
○ 9. One Conversation at a Time
Respect the facilitator and each other by allowing one person to speak at a time.
○ 10. End with Commitment to the Work
Leave each meeting with a shared understanding of next steps and a spirit of collaboration
as we move toward our common goal.
