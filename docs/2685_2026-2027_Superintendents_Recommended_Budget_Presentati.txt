RSU 5 2026-2027 FY 27
Tier 1 (~8%) : Key Considerations– Pros
● Preserves more current staffing and programming in the near term
● Reduces immediate disruption for students and staff
● Maintains MTSS Ed. Tech. support for General Education
Tier 1 (~8%) : Key Considerations– Cons
● Reduces financial flexibility in future years
● Increases the risk of larger or more abrupt reductions later
● Delays, rather than addresses, underlying structural cost pressures
● Makes long-term stabilization more difficult

RSU 5 2026-2027 FY 27
Why Tier 3 (~6%) is Not Recommended
● Significant, widespread staffing losses in critical areas (Tier 3 reductions are all staff)
● Severely limits flexibility to respond to special education, enrollment changes, and
unforeseen needs
● RTI reductions at PES would dramatically reduce student supports
● We COULD move PES Gr. 5 to MLS, but this creates transportation issues that we don’t have
have an answer for, and is a major structural (and likely one-year-only) change
● Removing TWO Unified Arts positions at FMS would severely compromise our ability to
meet student needs, especially in Health/Wellness
● Strong sense that the community would support keeping these positions
Reducing to This Level Is Disruptive and Unnecessary
