Meeting Notes
Freeport High School
Project:
August 3, 2016
Date:
Attendees: Lyndon Keck*, John Simoneau/Committee Chairman, Kelly Wentworth, Business Manager/RSU 5,
Ben Jamo, David Watts, Julie Coleman, Becky Foley, Superintendent/RSU 5, Jennifer Gulko, FHS
Principal, David Smail/FHS Physics, Craig Sickels/FHS Athletic Director and Tim Gidding
Purpose: Monthly Building Committee Meeting
These notes were prepared by Lyndon Keck to the best of his ability. If there are any oversights
please notify PDT Architects within three (3) working days.
Topic Agenda/Notes Action
1. The meeting was called to order by John Simoneau at 7:35 a.m. in the
cafeteria at the Freeport Middle School.
2. Lyndon passed out a copy of the agenda, a copy of Brook Plummer’s
monthly summary of construction progress, and copy of the spreadsheet for
requested building upgrades to Committee members.
3. John provided a summary of the status of the project. He explained
sheetrock was mostly finished on the entrance level and prime coat of
painting was occurring. He talked about the ceiling grid being put up in the
library and 100 hallway. He commented that circular lights had been
installed at the intersection outside the toilet room.
4. John explained asbestos abatement was done during July. He talked about
the men’s and women’s #100 restrooms where plumbing rough-in was
underway.
5. New Guidance Area had a number of hidden conditions. There are a
number of change orders related to utilities hidden in the walls which needed
to be moved to make the floor plan work. John mentioned the exterior brick
was mostly completed.
6. Two windows had failed in late June. These were repaired by Marvin,
retested, and passed inspection.
7. Testing windows now is more common than it used to be during
construction. Marvin is a quality window manufacturer.

8. Reviewed progress of electrical where LED lights had mostly been installed
in the classroom and new windows installed in the library.
9. John stated the schedule appeared be in good shape based on the report
from the construction superintendent at the pay requisition which had
happened on Tuesday 8/02/16.
10. Change Order #8 had been approved in June and Change Order #9 had
been approved in July. He gave a brief overview of the items included on
each change order.
11. John also explained some of the change orders related to shut off valves in
the #100 men’s and women’s restrooms, along with Dennis’ request to
upgrade ventilation in the bathrooms, and adding a drip edge for the
maintenance building.
12. Questions were asked about the window testing. Lyndon and John
explained the window testing is set up with a vacuum system that replicates
wind drive rain at 45-50 MPH over a sustained period. A question was
asked if it was the window unit that leaked or the installation was. John
explained it was the window unit itself which had been manufactured in the
factory and not the installation by Sheridan Corporation.
13. Lyndon gave the architect’s report of the progress of the work. He explained
the pay requisition current payment was $1,101,000 and this represented
62.6% completion of the entire project
14. Change Order #8 was $83,470. Change Order #9 was for $52,014. The
largest items on both change orders were for major items which had been
approved as “building upgrades” by the Building Committee. These included
access card readers at doors, kitchen changes requested by the Food
Service Director, and upgrades at the library learning commons
15. Lyndon talked about two items where final numbers had come in on the
requested building upgrade.
Item #3, final price for the library ceiling and light, was $74,019. The original
estimate which had been approved by the Building Committee was for
$75,000 +/-.
The other item was the electrical pathways for cameras, security system,
and access doors. The final price was $14,482. Lyndon explained the
Building Committee had previously approved $7,500 +/-.

16. Lyndon summarized prices which had been received from Sheridan
Corporation and Crooker Construction for items which the Building
Committee had requested. Lyndon explained adding additional landscape
plantings and mulch at the front of the building was estimated at $15,277;
new picnic tables and granite benches, as well as backless seats $13,700,
adding a concrete plaza behind the food court by the bus drop $4,590
Lyndon explained there was also a quote from Welch Signage to install an
exterior post and bracket system for banners on the front lawn. The quote
was for $4,465. He explained this item could be reviewed to see if a more
affordable system could be installed
17. The Building Committee confirmed they did not want to spend money on
additional landscaping at this time. Lyndon explained Crooker needed to
know if revisions were going to be made to the back plaza. Lyndon
explained there was an area approximately 32’ x 18’ which was intended to
be loam and grass seed. This was proposed by Pat Carroll to be a concrete
plaza so students sitting at the picnic tables and benches would not trample
the grass into a mud field.
18. Committee members preferred not to do the concrete and suggested doing a
stone dust product in lieu of the top topsoil and loam. PDT to investigate
with Pat Carroll and make a recommendation for a product.
19. There was a question about the FPAC door and piano garage and when the
door will be installed.
20. David Watts explained that, in his opinion, the work being done to the FPAC
was a “disaster” throughout the summer. Air conditioning was not
operational. There was a substantial amount of dust on the chairs as a
result of holes being put through the FPAC wall.
21. Lyndon explained that construction documents owned letters that said “Café
Freeport” on the back wall of the kitchen servery area. These are 12” high
brushed aluminum letters pin mounted on the herringbone pattern tile.
22. Committee members felt the lettering was not needed. There is so much
artwork in the space that it would be too confusing. People in the food
court/cafeteria should already know it is a café area. These are 12” high
brushed aluminum letters pin mounted on the herringbone pattern tile.
23. Lyndon showed a sketch and explained the contract documents own two (2)
sets of letters, 12” high brushed aluminum letters, pin mounted on the rain
canopies to either side of the new main entrance. Lyndon stated the design
team thought it was better to just have one set of letters and put it on the
