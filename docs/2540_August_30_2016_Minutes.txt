Meeting Notes
Freeport High School
Project:
August 30, 2016 at 7:30 a.m.
Date:
Attendees: Lyndon Keck*, John Simoneau/Committee Chairman, Kelly Wentworth, Business Manager/RSU 5,
Ben Jamo, David Watts, Julie Coleman, Becky Foley, Superintendent/RSU 5, Jennifer Gulko, Principal/
Freeport High School, David Smail/FHS Physics, and Kim LaMarre/Committee Member.
Purpose: Monthly Building Committee Meeting
These notes were prepared by Lyndon Keck to the best of his ability. If there are any oversights
please notify PDT Architects within three (3) working days.
Topic Agenda/Notes Action
1. The Building Committee’s first order of business was to convene at the front
entrance of the school and do a tour of the existing school, as well as the
new addition. Hardhats were provided for all. The tour was led by John
Simoneau and Lyndon Keck.
2. The tour went down Corridors 100 and 200. The tour included the new
nurse’s clinic, teacher’s room alterations, new bathrooms serving teachers
and health clinic, hallway light upgrades in the corridors, and classrooms
receiving renovated finishes.
3. Corridor 100 tour included new bathrooms with new finishes and new paint
on existing tile.
4. At the boy’s bathroom it was noted a privacy screen would need to be added
to block the view of the boy’s urinals.
5. At Future’s Lab 101 there was a question about how extensive the orange
paint would be along the three walls.
6. It was noted that the existing lockers being kept were in good condition,
except for the sloped locker tops which have been badly dented and
disfigured over the years. Lyndon mentioned that perhaps something could
be done to provide new tops for the lockers.
7. The group looked at typical classrooms with new carpet, new lights and
paint.
8. It was explained that the existing Whiteboards were being remounted
because the new Whiteboards would not arrive until the first or second week
of October.

9. It was noted that existing doors had not yet been replaced with new wood
doors and new hardware. This would be done over the next two months
during after school hours.
10. The tour visited the Learning Center which had new ceilings, lights, carpet
and paint, as well as the construction of two new seminar conference rooms.
11. The tour continued on to the guidance suite were it was noted the guidance
counselors had concerns about the fabric on the chairs being “too busy”.
There was general discussion about waiting until the rooms were 100%
complete before determining if chairs should be moved to other locations.
12. The tour went on into the new building where progress included a substantial
amount of new gypsum wallboard which was receiving tape and joint
compound. Painting was beginning for priming.
13. The tour went downstairs and went through the food court, kitchen servery,
fitness rooms, health room and music room. The Building Committee also
went outside and looked at the proposed grass and tree plaza at the back of
the cafeteria.
14. The base asphalt had been placed for the bus drop off and the student
parking lot.
15. The group looked at the potential location for Freeport High School signage
on the exterior cafeteria wall.
16. The group walked around to the front of the school and looked at the
signage on the existing Freeport Performing Arts Center, as well as the
existing painted wood sign above the administrative area.
17. The Building Committee went inside and had a brief update from John
Simoneau about the status of the work. He explained there would be a pay
requisition meeting later in the day. The requisition approved by the
architect was for $915,223 and represented 71.5% completion of the project.
In addition, Change Order #10, which contained thirteen (13) change
proposal items totaling $28,761 was approved by the Construction Team.
Individual change proposals approved by the Construction Team included
two credit deducts for eliminating gypsum wallboard soffit at a corridor and
changing the variable air volume systems for the kitchen hood. Additional
work approved included adding water shut-off valves for bathrooms,
additional failed glazing units in the 1960 classroom building, changing
