Returning to Music in RSU5: A Safe Approach


Referencing Orders and Guidelines from the Following Resources:
National Federation of State High School Associations (NFHS)
National Association for Music Education (NAfME)
Maine Music Educators Association (MMEA)
Maine Principals’ Association Sports Medicine Committee 


www.rsu5.org/music
  



Teachers and School Administrator(s) should hold an educational session prior to the first class/rehearsal to educate students of all rules related to playing and singing inside buildings and the requirements outlined below. 
For additional guidance regarding music and Covid-19, please reference the NFHS, NAfME, and MMEA webpages.  
* DISTANCING 
   * Social distancing guidelines of a minimum of 6x6 feet for all musicians, vocal and instrumental, with 6x9 feet for trombone players.  These distances apply to both indoor and outdoor ensemble performance.                                                                                         
   * * SPECTATORS/CAPACITY
   * VIRTUAL - At this time, all RSU5 concerts and events will be held virtually for the 2020-2021 school year.  No specific plan is being made for in-person performances for the Spring of 2021.
   * Teachers and school personnel are responsible for limiting the number of individuals that can gather in a shared space, in accordance with the Governor’s Executive Order on gathering size limits, currently set at 50 people indoors and 100 people outdoors, or fewer if distancing rules cannot be accommodated.


* FACE COVERINGS
   * Well-fitting masks must be worn by all students and staff in the music classroom.
   * Wind instrumental players must use bell covers that are two-layered.
   * Wind instrumental players must wear masks with slits for their mouths during rehearsal.
   * Singers must wear masks at all times.
   * When musicians are not actively singing or playing instruments, regular masks which fully cover the nose and mouth must be worn.
* SCREENING 
* It should be the understanding of all musicians involved that musicians and directors have completed a self-screening for signs and symptoms of COVID-19, prior to participation.




MUSIC REHEARSAL and CLASS CONSIDERATIONS


General / Classroom Music 
   * Social distancing of at least 6 feet during singing and wind playing activities should always be maintained between class participants, whether seated or standing. 
   * All musicians should face the same direction to the extent possible. 
   * Students must be assigned instruments for their sole use; students may not share instruments or instrument equipment as well as--  
      * General music teachers should consider providing each student with a personal music kit that includes common classroom instruments (rhythm sticks, mallets, etc.).
      * Percussion, string and non-wind instruments; no sharing is preferred. However if shared, a cleaning should occur between each use. 
      * Students must not share music stands.
      * Students must not share sheet music. Additional sheet music may need to be purchased to ensure all students have their own copies. 
      * Students must not share any other print class material or books. Individual copies must be provided.
   * Good hygiene norms should be followed, and school approved disinfection strategies should be used.
   * Training and proper PPE must be provided to those who are responsible for cleaning.


Instrumental including Winds (Band, Jazz Band, Small Ensembles)   
   * Social distancing of at least 6 feet (9 feet trombones) should always be maintained between class participants, whether seated or standing. 
   * Students and adults must wear well fitting face coverings at all times. Masks with a slit for a mouthpiece must be used when playing brass or woodwind instruments. Masks should be two-layered.
   * Two-layered nylon or cloth bell coverings must be used on all wind instruments.
   * All musicians should face the same direction to the extent possible. 
   * Class size will be informed by rehearsal room size, distancing requirements and ventilation.
   * Good hygiene norms should be followed, and school approved disinfection strategies should be used.
   * Training and proper PPE must be provided to those who are responsible for cleaning.
   * Students must be assigned instruments for their sole use; students may not share instruments or instrument equipment (reeds, mouthpieces, oils, wax, etc.) as well as--
      * Percussion, string and non-wind instruments; no sharing is preferred.  However if shared, a cleaning should occur between each use.
      * Percussion students must not share mallets without properly disinfecting them--or students can wear gloves.
      * Students must not share music stands.
      * Students must not share sheet music. Additional sheet music may need to be purchased to ensure all students have their own copies. 
      * Students must not share any other print class material or books. Individual copies must be provided.


Instrumental without Winds (Guitar, Piano, Percussion) 
   * Social distancing of at least 3 feet should always be maintained between class participants, whether seated or standing. 
   * Students must be assigned instruments for their sole use; students may not share instruments or instrument equipment (picks, etc.) as well as--
      * Percussion, string and non-wind instruments; no sharing is preferred.  However if shared, a cleaning should occur between each use.
      * Percussion students must not share mallets without properly disinfecting them--or students can wear gloves.
      * Students must not share music stands.
      * Students must not share sheet music. Additional sheet music may need to be purchased to ensure all students have their own copies.
      * Students must not share any other print class material or books. Individual copies must be provided.
   * Students and adults must wear well fitting face coverings at all times.
   * All musicians should face the same direction to the extent possible
   * Class size will be informed by rehearsal room size, distancing requirements and ventilation, including at least.
   * Good hygiene norms should be followed, and school approved disinfection strategies should be used.
   * Training and proper PPE must be provided to those who are responsible for cleaning.


Chorus
   * Social distancing of at least 6 feet should always be maintained between class participants, whether seated or standing. 
      * Students must not share equipment or instruments
      * Students must not share music stands.
      * Students must not share sheet music. Additional sheet music may need to be purchased to ensure all students have their own copies.
      * Students must not share any other print class material or books. Individual copies must be provided.

What does this look like in our RSU5 schools?
Morse Street School - Rachel Domin
General Music:  Music is currently taking place inside the classroom for 30 minutes.    Students will sing (masked) at least 6 feet apart for up to 15 minutes.  6 foot minimum is ample spacing for the vocal projection of the younger singers. The remainder of the class will continue to use percussion instruments, movement, rhythm reading and writing and  listening exercises.  
Mast Landing School - Ben Potvin & Rachel Domin
General Music:  Music is currently taking place outside in the parking lot.  Students will sing (masked) outside at 6 feet due to an inability to socially distance in the music classroom.  Wind instruments (recorder) will not take place this year.  
Band:  Students will practice (masked and bell cover on instrument) in the music room and MLS gym to allow for smaller groups and 10-foot-distancing.  Masks and bell covers are washed at school and returned to the student at the next lesson.
Chorus:  Students will sing (masked) in the MLS gym to allow for 10-foot-distancing.  
Durham Community School - Jill Palmer & Allie Edwards
General Music:  Music is currently taking place inside the classroom for 50 minutes.    Students will sing (masked) at 6-8 feet inside the classroom for a maximum of 15 minutes.  The remainder of the class will continue to use percussion instruments, movement and a listening exercise.  Wind instruments (recorder) will not take place this year for Grades 3-4. 
Band:   Middle school students meet once a week for in person band lessons, and virtually for their individual band lesson. I will be working to move some of these lessons in person before school. Beginning band students are meeting in two small groups once a week for like instrument band lessons. Small like instrument lessons will be offered before school. Students will always wear masks purchased, as well as bell covers for their instruments. Students will also always maintain 10 feet of space between players. Band takes places on the stage. 
Chorus:  If allowed to sing, students will utilize the gym or stage space, singing, masked and 10 feet apart. 
Pownal Elementary School - Allie Edwards
General Music:   Students will continue to socially distance in music class indoors. Percussion, and stringed instruments will continue to be used as well as masked singing 6 feet apart. 
Band:   Students will continue to wear masks, and bell covers whenever playing. Students will also maintain at least 6 feet of distance between. 
Chorus:  Students would have chorus socially distanced while wearing masks at least 10 feet apart.  
Freeport Middle School - Erin York & Nate Menifield
General Music:   Students will continue to be instructed with current classroom guidelines (3-feet apart and wearing a mask). Percussion instruments will continue to be sanitized before and after each use.
Band: Band students meet in the FMS band room one day a week for a lesson. Students are spaced 10-feet apart, wearing masks, and instruments have bell covers. Sheet music is presented on a screen whenever possible to limit the use of music stands and paper. There is an air purifier in the room as well as an exhaust fan. Masks and bell covers remain in student cases and are put through two wash cycles by the teacher every two weeks. FMS band students will begin meeting as an ensemble outside once the weather is warmer. Masks and bell covers will continue to be utilized. 
Chorus:  Chorus students will continue to meet with their designated cohort in the FMS chorus room once per week for 35 minutes. Any singing-oriented activities will occur with students spaced 10 feet apart, masked, and facing the same direction (for larger cohorts, the gym may be used when necessary). The room contains an air purifier and multiple windows; and all students will be expected to continue abiding by standard sanitizing practices. As is currently the case, each student will receive their own copy of all instructional materials to minimize the risk of cross-contamination. 
Freeport High School - Shawn McKeown & Nate Menifield
Piano:   Students will be playing piano independently on digital keyboards in the keyboard lab. There will be an air purifier in the room, and there is only a maximum of one block period of piano on any given day. Students use hand sanitizer before and after playing, and also wipe down the instruments carefully and thoroughly after each use. Students are expected to follow all FHS safety guidelines.
Guitar:   Students will continue to be instructed with classroom guitars, as they did in the fall semester. Students are expected to sanitize their hands before and after playing, and they are also expected to wipe down their instruments on an as-needed basis (to protect the quality of the instrument materials). Students are expected to follow all FHS safety guidelines.
Music Technology:         Students will continue to be instructed by the course instructor in class, and will work independently on their own laptops. Students are expected to follow all FHS safety guidelines.
Band:   Band students will be meeting with their cohorts during class once a week (either Monday or Tuesday), from 1:10pm-2:30pm. Students are not only expected to follow all FHS safety guidelines, but they will also be expected to be spaced a minimum of at least 6 feet apart, wearing masks, and have bell covers. As the current plan stands, approximately half of each cohort (approximately 10-15 students) will practice in the FHS Chorus Room, and then the other half will practice in the FHS Band Room. Both rooms have more than adequate ventilation, as well as windows that can be opened, and air purifiers that will remain on 24/7. FHS Band students will look into meeting as a larger ensemble outside, once the weather is warmer. 
Chorus:  Chorus students will continue to meet with their designated cohort during class once per week for 75 minutes. Singing-focused portions of class will occur in the FHS auditorium, with all students spaced 10 feet apart, masked, and facing the same direction. Non-singing music instruction will take place in the FHS chorus room, in accordance with all FHS safety guidelines. As noted above, both spaces are adequately ventilated, and all students will be expected to continue abiding by standard sanitizing practices. As is currently the case, each student will receive their own copy of all instructional materials to minimize the risk of cross-contamination.
