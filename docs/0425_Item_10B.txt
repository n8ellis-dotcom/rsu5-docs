FHS Plan for 5 Day a Week in Person Learning
Schedules:
● Return to Maroon/White schedule with an abbreviated daily schedule
● No scheduled lunch period: school day goes from 7:50am to 1:15pm
○ Period 1/2: 7:50-8:55
○ Period 3/4: 9:00-10:05
○ Advisory: 10:10-10:55
○ Period 6/7: 11:00-12:05
○ Period 8/9: 12:10-1:15
○ Staff Lunch: 1:15-1:45 (students take a bag lunch home or eat in the cafe)
○ Faculty Prep Time/Office Hours: 1:45-2:40
■ Optional study hall for any student and student-athletes: 1:45-2:30
● Supervised by Ed Techs
■ Prep Time for Teachers
● Afternoon Prep (1:45-2:40): 55 min x 5 = 275 min/week
● PLC/Prep during the day - supervise or coverage when needed
○ Coverage for 30 min; 30 min prep (30 min x 3 = 90 min)
○ If no coverage needed, 65 min prep (65 x 5 = 325 min)
● Total: 510 min/week if coverage needed, 600 min/week if not
○ Currently 375 min/week
Physical Space:
● Classrooms: enough space for 3 feet between students and 6 feet between
teacher/students
○ Every room was measured to determine capacity
○ Teachers have the option of:
■ Keeping all students in the classroom if they can maintain 3 ft
■ Moving to a larger classroom
■ Sending students to an “overflow” area and teaching synchronously (no
teacher has chosen this option thus far)
○ Windows will be opened as much as possible
○ Air filter on
○ Doors open to increase ventilation
● Classes go outside when possible (set up outdoor classrooms again)
● Some seniors will leave at the start of May for Senior Projects, others at the end of May
● Passing Periods:
○ Students enter and exit the door closest to their classrooms
○ Continue with limits on number of students in the bathrooms
○ Guidance hallway is one way only during passing periods

Arrival:
● Students from the bus goes to the Cafe or FPAC
● Other students outside until 7:40am, (can be in cafe or FPAC lobby if need be)
● Students go straight to class - no walking laps inside (walk outside)
● Hallway by guidance area one way during all passing periods (open during class time)
● Late arrival and early dismissal for all grades, reduces numbers in study halls (1/2 & 8/9
only)
○ Grade qualifications required for early dismissal/late arrival for all grades
● Open campus for juniors and seniors only (Grade qualifications remain)
● Study hall is moved to cafeteria, use Ipad to sign in
○ Allows for study hall room to be open for class use
Dismissal:
● First bus run at 1:30 (gives students 15 minutes to get lunch food before bus arrives)
● Second run at 2:30 (“late bus” allows students to stay for extra help)
○ 1:50 - 2:30pm - optional study hall / homework help in the cafeteria
Nutrition:
● No changes to the breakfast schedule and procedure.
● Kitchen will be open from breakfast to after school bus departure time.
○ Eating during study halls, guided study halls and advisory study hall
○ A la carte and fruit available all periods of the day
○ Food served at 1:15 pm dismissal, prior to the 1:30 pm bus departure
○ Breakfast and lunch items, along with a la carte snacks, will be available
throughout the full serving time period
○ Supervised lunch available from 1:15-1:45
○ Any students who have not eaten and are taking the bus will go to the front of the
line at 1:15 dismissal
○ Signs will be posted at exit doors to remind students to visit the cafeteria prior to
leaving school grounds if they have not yet eaten
○ Students can have a snack during times when passing classes outside and/or
when a teacher takes students outside for a snack or mask break
● Capacity of the cafeteria is 150 on 3/26/2021
○ 6 foot distance will be maintained at all times while students are eating
Remote Students:
● Fully remote students in FHS classes will still need synchronous instruction
○ Working to move fully remote students into the same section so there are fewer
synchronous classes
○ Hired two teachers to alleviate some synchronous teaching requirements
○ Offering to have remote students come in for some classes

Preparation for April 5:
● Two full planning days before we start: (students work independently at home)
○ March 31st (early release, some planning time, some meeting time)
○ April 1st: full planning day
○ April 2nd: full planning day
○ April 5th: All students back 5 days
Region 10 Students:
● AM and PM Region 10 schedule will still work (both will miss advisory)
● Region 10 remains at 2 days/ week
○ Students will have study halls on “off” days or come in late/go home if they have
transportation (van transportation where possible)
○ Will still have transportation at 2:30 if afternoon Reg. 10 student
Advisory:
● Continue with callbacks and cafeteria study hall for advisees
● Open track when weather permits
● Clubs can meet in person, larger clubs can meet in FPAC lobby
● Advisors can take students outside in the first 10 minutes or during advisory if no
callbacks (mask and snack break)
