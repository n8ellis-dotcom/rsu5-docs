RSU 5 Early Childhood Education Task Force – Meeting #1 Agenda
Date: Wednesday, October 29, 2025
Time: 5:30 – 7:30 p.m.
Location: Morse Street School
Meeting Goals
● Build a shared understanding of the state’s early childhood education mandate and our
district’s responsibilities.
● Take stock of current district and community resources.
● Identify key areas of concern and opportunity.
● Clarify next steps, including opportunities to join state cohorts for early implementation.
Agenda
5:30 – 5:40 | Welcome and Introductions
● Superintendent’s opening remarks and purpose of the Task Force.
● Roundtable introductions (name, role, and connection to early childhood education).
● Overview of meeting norms and collaboration expectations;
○ 1. Stay Student-Centered
Keep our focus on what will best serve children and families — especially our youngest
learners — as we explore options for the district’s future responsibilities.
○ 2. Honor All Perspectives
We come from different roles and experiences. Listen with curiosity and respect; seek to
understand before seeking to be understood.
○ 3. Assume Positive Intent
Everyone in the room is here because they care about children and want to do what’s right.
Approach differences in opinion with generosity.
○ 4. Speak from Experience, Listen for Understanding
Use “I” statements to share your perspective. When others speak, listen to understand, not to
reply.

○ 5. Be Present and Engaged
Limit side conversations, multitasking, and phone use. We have important work to do in a
short time.
○ 6. Keep It Constructive and Solution-Oriented
Our charge is to identify possibilities and options — not to assign blame. Let’s focus on what
we can do and what’s within our control.
○ 7. Be Mindful of Time and Space
Help ensure everyone’s voice is heard by being concise when speaking and inviting quieter
voices in.
○ 8. Transparency and Trust
We will share information openly and ensure that processes are clear to all. What we learn
together will be communicated accurately and respectfully.
○ 9. One Conversation at a Time
Respect the facilitator and each other by allowing one person to speak at a time.
○ 10. End with Commitment to the Work
Leave each meeting with a shared understanding of next steps and a spirit of collaboration
as we move toward our common goal.
5:40 – 6:10 | Understanding the Mandate
● Presentation: State requirements for providing FAPE (Free Appropriate Public Education)
for children ages 3–5 by July 1, 2028.
● Overview of Cohort 3 and Cohort 4 options:
○ Cohort 3: begins FY26, districts assume responsibility for 4-year-olds.
○ Cohort 4: begins FY27, districts assume responsibility for both 3- and 4-year-olds.
○ Funding opportunities and considerations for each.
● Brief Q&A.
6:10 – 6:40 | Taking Stock of What We Have
● Group discussion: Inventory of current resources and strengths in the district and
community.
○ Pre-K programs (half-day, staffing, facilities).
○ Community child care and early learning providers.

○ Partnerships and service delivery models.
● Recorder captures shared assets and gaps on a visual chart or whiteboard.
6:40 – 7:10 | Identifying Primary Areas of Concern
● Small group discussion (mixed stakeholder tables):
○ What are your biggest questions or concerns about meeting this new responsibility?
○ What opportunities do you see to build on existing strengths?
● Large group debrief: share out themes.
● Begin to identify areas for deeper exploration in future meetings.
7:10 – 7:25 | Looking Ahead: Framing the Work of the Task Force
● Overview of the four-meeting timeline and expected outcomes:
○ Meeting 1 – Understanding the landscape
○ Meeting 2 – Exploring models and scenarios
○ Meeting 3 – Evaluating feasibility and costs
○ Meeting 4 – Developing the menu of options for the Board
● Confirm next meeting date and pre-reading materials.
7:25 – 7:30 | Closing Reflections
● Quick round: one takeaway or hope for the work ahead.
● Thank you and adjourn.
